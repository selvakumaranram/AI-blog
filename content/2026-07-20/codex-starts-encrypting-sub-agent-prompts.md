The development platform Codex has implemented a new security measure involving the encryption of prompts sent to sub-agents. This update aims to protect the integrity and confidentiality of internal instructions processed during automated tasks. Developers utilizing these systems will now see an added layer of obfuscation for their background operational commands.

**Why it matters:** This change highlights a growing industry focus on securing the communication pathways between AI agents to prevent potential prompt injection or data leakage. It serves as a reminder that infrastructure security is becoming a critical component of agentic AI deployments.
