Federal aviation regulators have restored Boeing's authority to independently verify the safety standards of its 737 MAX and 787 aircraft models. This decision reverses a previous limitation that required government officials to personally approve every individual plane before delivery. The manufacturer will now resume its internal certification processes under federal oversight.

**Why it matters:** As Boeing increasingly integrates automated systems and AI-driven diagnostic tools into its manufacturing and quality control pipelines, the return of internal certification authority highlights the critical intersection of software-reliant safety protocols and regulatory trust.
