The privacy-focused photo storage service Ente has officially transitioned to an open-source model. This shift allows the public to inspect the company's entire codebase and infrastructure architecture. By making these resources transparent, the organization aims to foster greater trust and community collaboration.

**Why it matters:** As AI-driven photo management becomes more prevalent, this move toward radical transparency provides a benchmark for how privacy-centric companies can prove their security claims to users.
