New York City officials are considering a mandate that would force property managers and real estate agents to declare when artificial intelligence is utilized in their housing advertisements. This proposal aims to increase transparency regarding the digital manipulation of property photos and listing descriptions. The potential regulation seeks to prevent prospective tenants and buyers from being misled by computer-generated enhancements.

**Why it matters:** This development highlights the growing legislative push to regulate AI-generated content in sectors where consumer trust and accurate representation are critical. It serves as a bellwether for how local governments may begin enforcing disclosure standards for automated tools in commercial markets.
